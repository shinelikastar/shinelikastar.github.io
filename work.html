research

last summer, i was one of ten recipients of the National Science Foundation Research Experiences for Undergraduates MEDical Informatics Program. i worked closely with Dr. Timothy Carroll and his group at the Imaging Research Institute at the University of Chicago on the correlation of genetic information and radiomic features. i developed scripts in Matlab to automate a pipeline to extract gene expression of glioblastoma multiforme from geometric features of MRI. as a result of this research, i co-authored two abstracts. both are pending review for the SPIE Medical Imaging Symposium and International Society Magnetic Resonance in Medicine (ISMRM) conference.

writing

most of my work is centered around food. i'm interested in how food forges identity: physical, gendered, cultural, political. my writing explores how eating and cooking creates connections or boundaries.

somewhere in between

though the disciplines i'm studying may seem disjoint, i'm deeply interested in the intersection of language, art, & science. currently, i am seeking opportunities to create as a means for bringing personal and community healing, insight, and justice.
